🧠 know your prompts
tap any technique to learn how to use it
🎭Role Prompting+
Tell the AI who to be before giving the task. Sets expertise, tone and perspective.

example
"You are a senior UX designer with 10 years of experience..."
📌Instruction+
The core ask. Be direct, specific and action-oriented. Vague instructions get vague answers.

example
"Write a 3-point summary in plain English, each point under 20 words."
🌐Context+
Give the AI the background it needs — audience, situation, constraints.

example
"The audience is non-technical managers in a 50-person company."
📥Input+
Provide the data or content the AI should work with.

example
"Here is the email thread: [paste text]..."
📤Output Format+
Tell the AI exactly how to structure the response.

example
"Return as a numbered list. Max 5 items. No intro paragraph."
🔗Chain-of-Thought+
Ask the AI to reason step by step before answering.

example
"Think through this carefully before giving your final answer."
📋Few-Shot+
Show the AI 1–3 examples of good responses before asking.

example
"Here are two examples of the style I want: [ex1] / [ex2]..."
🎯Specificity+
Replace vague words with precise numbers, names and constraints.

weak → strong
"Write something short" → "Write exactly 3 sentences, under 40 words total."
🗣️Tone & Voice+
Tell the AI the emotional register you want.

example
"Use a warm, conversational tone. Avoid corporate jargon."
🚫Constraints+
Tell the AI what to avoid or exclude.

example
"Do not use bullet points. Do not mention pricing."
🎪Audience Framing+
Tell the AI who will read the output.

example
"Explain this to a non-technical founder who understands business but not engineering."
⚡Zero-Shot+
Giving the AI a task with no examples — relying purely on instruction, context and role.

vs few-shot
Use zero-shot for clear tasks, few-shot when you need a specific style.
🪞Self-Reflection+
Ask the AI to review and critique its own output before finishing.

example
"After writing your answer, review it for clarity and accuracy."
🔁Iteration+
Great prompts are built in layers. Start with a draft, see what is missing, then refine.

tip
Ask yourself: "What would the AI need to know to nail this?" — then add those details.
