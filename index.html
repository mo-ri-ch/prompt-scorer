🧠 know your prompts
tap any technique to learn how to use it
🎭Role Prompting+
Tell the AI who to be before giving the task. Sets expertise, tone and perspective.

example
"You are a senior UX designer with 10 years of experience..."
📌Instruction+
The core ask. Be direct, specific and action-oriented. Vague instructions get vague answers.

example
"Write a 3-point summary in plain English, each point under 20 words."
🌐Context+
Give the AI the background it needs — audience, situation, constraints.

example
"The audience is non-technical managers in a 50-person company."
📥Input+
Provide the data or content the AI should work with.

example
"Here is the email thread: [paste text]..."
📤Output Format+
Tell the AI exactly how to structure the response.

example
"Return as a numbered list. Max 5 items. No intro paragraph."
🔗Chain-of-Thought+
Ask the AI to reason step by step before answering.

example
"Think through this carefully before giving your final answer."
📋Few-Shot+
Show the AI 1–3 examples of good responses before asking.

example
"Here are two examples of the style I want: [ex1] / [ex2]..."
🎯Specificity+
Replace vague words with precise numbers, names and constraints.

weak → strong
"Write something short" → "Write exactly 3 sentences, under 40 words total."
🗣️Tone & Voice+
Tell the AI the emotional register you want.

example
"Use a warm, conversational tone. Avoid corporate jargon."
🚫Constraints+
Tell the AI what to avoid or exclude.

example
"Do not use bullet points. Do not mention pricing."
🎪Audience Framing+
Tell the AI who will read the output.

example
"Explain this to a non-technical founder who understands business but not engineering."
⚡Zero-Shot+
Giving the AI a task with no examples — relying purely on instruction, context and role.

vs few-shot
Use zero-shot for clear tasks, few-shot when you need a specific style.
🪞Self-Reflection+
Ask the AI to review and critique its own output before finishing.

example
"After writing your answer, review it for clarity and accuracy."
🔁Iteration+
Great prompts are built in layers. Start with a draft, see what is missing, then refine.

tip
Ask yourself: "What would the AI need to know to nail this?" — then add those details.

Task: Write a leave request email to your manager
Writing
❌ weak prompt
Write an email asking for leave.
No role, no context, no tone, no format. AI guesses everything — result is generic.
✅ strong prompt
You are a professional employee writing to your direct manager. Write a polite, concise email requesting 3 days of leave next week for a family function. Tone: formal but warm. Keep it under 80 words. Include: reason, dates, and a line offering to hand over pending tasks.
Role + instruction + context + output format + constraints. Every detail guided.

Task: Explain how AI works to a non-technical person
Explaining
❌ weak prompt
Explain AI.
One word topic, no audience, no format. Output will be textbook-style and overwhelming.
✅ strong prompt
Explain how AI works to a 45-year-old small business owner who has never studied technology. Use a simple analogy — compare it to something from everyday life. Keep it under 150 words. Avoid all technical jargon. End with one practical example of how they could use AI in their business today.
Audience framing + analogy instruction + word limit + jargon constraint + concrete output.
notice how much clearer the second response is

Task: Write an Instagram caption for a product
Marketing
❌ weak prompt
Write an Instagram caption for my coffee shop.
No tone, no product, no audience, no CTA. AI invents everything — rarely matches your brand.
✅ strong prompt
Write an Instagram caption for a cozy, independent coffee shop in Kochi targeting working professionals aged 25–35. We just launched a new cold brew. Tone: warm, a little witty, not corporate. Max 3 lines. End with a question to drive comments. Include 3–5 relevant hashtags.
Target audience + product + tone + format + CTA + hashtags. Nothing left to guesswork.

Task: Get feedback on a business idea
Analysis
❌ weak prompt
Is my business idea good? I want to sell handmade soaps.
No structure requested, no depth, yes/no framing. AI gives vague encouragement.
✅ strong prompt
Act as a startup mentor. Critically evaluate this business idea: selling premium handmade soaps online in India, targeting urban women aged 22–40. Think step by step. Cover: market opportunity, key risks, 3 things I must validate before starting, and one insight I probably haven't considered. Be honest, not encouraging.
Role + chain-of-thought + structured output + honest constraint. Forces real analysis.
chain-of-thought + role = dramatically deeper analysis

Task: Get UI copy for a signup button
UX Writing
❌ weak prompt
Give me text for a signup button.
No product context, no tone, no constraints. You will get "Sign Up" — you already knew that.
✅ strong prompt
You are a UX writer for a productivity app targeting busy freelancers. Write 5 variations of a primary CTA button label for the signup page. The tone should be action-oriented and friendly — not pushy. Each option must be under 4 words. Avoid generic phrases like "Sign Up" or "Get Started". Format: numbered list.
Role + 5 variations + tone + word constraint + exclusions + output format. Instantly usable.
constraints + exclusions = creative, usable options

Task: Write a short story opening
Creative
❌ weak prompt
Write a story about a detective.
Genre, setting, tone, length, style — all undefined. Result will be clichéd and forgettable.
✅ strong prompt
Write the opening paragraph (max 120 words) of a noir detective story set in 1940s Mumbai during monsoon season. The detective is a tired, middle-aged woman who has seen too much. Start in the middle of a scene — she is standing outside a closed door. Use sensory detail. Avoid clichés like "It was a dark and stormy night."
Setting + character + constraints + scene direction + sensory instruction + specific exclusion.
specificity turns generic AI writing into something memorable
